(1) What is the independent variable? What is the dependent variable?

Independent Variable - is the congruent words condition (where the words being displayed are color words whose names match the colors in which they are printed) and the incongruent words condition (where the words displayed are color words whose names do not match the colors in which they are printed)

Dependent Variable - is the time it takes to name the ink colors in equally-sized lists.

(2) What is an appropriate set of hypotheses for this task? Specify your null and alternative hypotheses, and clearly define any notation used. Justify your choices.

Null Hypothesis - The mean difference observed between the response time for the two groups with equally-sized lists, that is Congruent words and Incongruent words, is Zero.

$$H0: μ1 = μ2$$

Alternative Hypothesis - The mean difference observed between the response time for the two groups with equally-sized lists, that is Congruent words and Incongruent words, is NOT Zero.

$$H1: μ1 ≠ μ2$$

A dependent t-test is carried out, which compares the mean of the paired groups. Similar subjects are tested for both Congruent and Incongruent words.

As stated above, the Null Hypothesis suggests that there is no statistically significant difference between the mean times taken to name ink color. If the result obtained is significant it means, we can accept the alternative or reject the null.

t-statistics can be calculated using the equation of Signal to Noise ratio (Average Difference to Measure of Variability):

$$t_{statistic} = \frac{\bar x_{D} - \mu_0}{s_{D}/\sqrt{n}}$$

The degreesOfFreedom can be obtained using: the size of data minus 1. (n -1)

Significance level to be used is 0.05 (5% chance of making Type I error).

from math import sqrt
n = 24
### Hypothesis will be tested using two-tailed test with a significance level of 0.05 (i.e., 0.025 will be allocated on each tail)
degreesOfFreedom = n - 1 
sdf = 0.05 #significance difference 
confidence_level = 1 - sdf


################################################################################
#To calculate the t_statistics, find the mean of the difference 
difference_mean = (df['Congruent'] - df['Incongruent']).mean()
#To calculate the t_statistics, find the mean of deviation 
difference_deviation = (df['Congruent'] - df['Incongruent']).std()
#The t-value is: 
t_val = difference_mean/(difference_deviation/float(sqrt(24)))
print("Signal to Noise ratio or the t_statistics is: {:.4f}" .format(t_val))

Signal to Noise ratio or the t_statistics is: -8.0207
