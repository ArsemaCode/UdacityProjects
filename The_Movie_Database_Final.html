QUESTION 1 - Describe the correlation between High Budget and High Popularity - for the top hundred expensive movies.¶

Answer 1:¶

From the modified dataframe (df_expensive), the top ten expensive movies or movies with the highest budget were taken as a sample to investigate the correlation between producing expensive movies and their popularity among viewers. In most cases, production companies and movie crew anticipate that their high budget movies will be high grossing too. However from the Mdb used here, budget is not a sole factor for the popularity of a movie or its revenue. Movies can be very costly due to the popularity of the cast, the investment of the settings, genres, storyline and promotions(trailers, TV ads, billboards and websites). Hence, there is a very weak correlation between 'how much a movie grossed?' and 'how much budget was allocated?'.

Accordingly, further investigation was made to see if popularity reflects revenue. Looking at the scatter plot in Fig 2 of revenue vs popularity, there is a relatively stronger positive correlation. There are anomalies such as "Jurassic World", a movie with the highest popularity in the dataframe but a relatively low budget.

Fig 3 depict that the correlation between budget and revenue is positive, but not necessarily strong. Which can mean, not all high grossing movies required high budget in making them. "Jurassic World" is once again an anomaly. Hence, it is fair to assume that, it is an exceptional case in the film industry or there has been an error during data collection. Please note that, all the comparisons with/between budget and revenue were made under the assumption that both are given in USD.

QUESTION 2 - How does vote_count relate with revenue and budget?¶

Answer:¶

As illustrated in the scatterplot below, both revenue and budget have a positive correlation with vote_count. Moreover, it can be concluded from the scatterplot that revenue has a stronger positive correlation with vote_count. And this is the traditional assumption made by viewers or movie critiques. That is, if a movie has a good vote_count, then it will have more viewers;thus higher revenue.

There are some anomalies, especially movies with the top 3 highest revenues but not necessarily highest vote_count.

QUESTION 3 - Which top genres brought in the highest revenue?¶

Answer:¶

The horizontal bar plot below clearly shows that Drama has the highest total number of occurrences in the given dataset. Is this enough to conclude that it also brings in the highest revenue? That is illustrated using Seaborn's swarmplot.

From the swarmplot (based on the dataset used) it can be infered that most of the highest revenues come from genres such as Action, Adventure, Science Fiction, Thriller, Fantasy and Crime; making them the most popular genres among viewers. In the second place are, Western, Drama, Family, Animation, Comedy, Mystery and Romance. Leaving the last place, War, History, Music, Horror, Documentary and Foreign movies as the least popular interms of their revenue. This was further explored using a horizontal bar chart.

The result calls for a future work on finding out how if the popularity of genres has evolved over the years as the societal makeup, values and the role of technology has seen a significant change.
